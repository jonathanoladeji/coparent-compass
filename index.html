    You Are Not Alone

    Parental alienation is one of the most painful aspects of high-conflict separation. These strategies are used by fathers worldwide to protect their relationship with their children.

    📱
    Centralize All Communication to One Platform
    ▼

    Moving everything to a dedicated co-parenting app creates a legally admissible, tamper-proof record.

    • OurFamilyWizard -- Court-admissible logs, read receipts, shared calendar.
    • TalkingParents -- Free tier. Every message timestamped and uneditable.
    • Fayr -- Combines messaging with expense tracking.
    • 2houses -- Popular in Europe and Africa.
    • Send one firm message: "Going forward, all co-parenting communication will be through [App]."
    • If they refuse to join, document that refusal as evidence of bad faith.
    📷
    Document Everything Systematically
    ▼
    • Keep a dated log of every missed visit, denied call, or interference incident.
    • Screenshot and back up every relevant message before blocking can occur.
    • After each call with your child, note duration, content, and anything notable.
    • Take photos during your parenting time -- timestamped proof you were present.
    • Request to be added to all school, medical, and activity communication lists independently.
    ⚖️
    Know Your Legal Rights -- Then Use Them
    ▼
    • File a contempt motion promptly if court-ordered parenting time is withheld.
    • Request a Parenting Coordinator in high-conflict situations.
    • Ask about a right of first refusal clause for childcare.
    • Request school and medical records independently -- both parents typically have this right.
    • Consider a Guardian ad Litem if alienation is severe.
    ❤️
    Maintain Consistent Low-Drama Contact
    ▼
    • Set a fixed "dad calls" schedule -- same day, same time. Make it a ritual.
    • Send physical mail -- letters, drawings, small gifts. Cannot be blocked or muted.
    • Never badmouth the other parent to your children, even when provoked.
    • Stay connected to their school, coaches, and friends' parents independently.
    🧠
    Protect Your Own Mental Health
    ▼
    • Find a therapist familiar with high-conflict co-parenting.
    • Join a fathers' support group: Fathers4Justice (UK), DadsDivorce (US), Families Need Fathers.
    • Set firm limits on when you check co-parenting messages.
    • Keep a personal emotional journal separate from your legal documentation log.
    On These Strategies Practical tools, not guarantees. The goal is always the wellbeing of your children.
